Tomato Sushi

Description

Tomato Nigiri is a fascinating recipe that has an appearance like Tuna Nigiri. We will be taking advantage of all the glumates present in Tomatoes and the magic of marinade to create a surprisingly savory and tender piece of sushi. You don't have to forgoe sushi just because you are inflicted with the desire to eat plant based. Whether for health, environmentalism, sympathy for animals, clout in progressive spaces, or a wondrous combination of these reasons, eating vegan can be culinarily satisfying.

What you'll need:

  • 2 ripe tomatoes, ideally Heirloom and fresh from a garden
  • 2 tablespoons of light soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of rice vinegar
  • 12 capers and 1 teaspoon of their juice
  • 2 tablespoons of a high-quality finishing olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • Nori, 2 pieces
  • Tupperware that holdsat least 2 cups
  • Cooking vessel, that can boil 2 tomatoes
  • bowl of ice water
  • sieve with handle
  • sharp knife

What you'll do:

  1. Score an "X" in the tomatoes' skins. Then blanche the two tomatoes for 1 minute in boiling water in the cooking vessel.
  2. With a handled sieve or a spider, reoe the tomatoes and immediately place into an ice water bath for at least 1 minute.
  3. Peel off the skins and slice the tomatoes, removing the seeds and inner part, slicing the meaty outer flesh.
  4. Put the soy sauce in the tupperware and layer in one nori, then neatly layer the tomatoe slices on top.
  5. put in capers and rice vinegar and suger and salt, then top with second nori piece.
  6. Leave this tupperware in the frusge for at least an hour.
  7. Serve this as desired, with or ithout sushi rice.
